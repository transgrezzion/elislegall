The Formation of the Kenyan Constitution: A Structural Analysis

By Osiris | Legal Intelligence Division

The constitutional journey of Kenya is a testament to the nation's quest for sovereignty, justice, and democratic governance. From the independence constitution of 1963 to the transformative 2010 framework, the formation process reflects the evolving needs of the Kenyan people.

The Constitutional Framework

A constitution serves as the supreme law of the Republic. Under the current Kenyan legal system, any formation or significant amendment is governed strictly by Chapter 16 of the Constitution.

Key Pillars of Formation

  • Sovereignty of the People: All political power belongs to the people of Kenya.
  • The Supremacy of the Constitution: Any law inconsistent with the Constitution is void to the extent of the inconsistency.
  • Devolved Government: The distribution of power between the National Government and the 47 County Governments.

The Process of Reform

Whether through a Parliamentary Initiative or a Popular Initiative, the formation of new constitutional clauses requires rigorous public participation and, in specific cases, a national referendum.
