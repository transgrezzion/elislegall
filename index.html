The Formation of the Constitution of Kenya 2010

The journey toward the 2010 Constitution was a decades-long struggle for reform. It began with the Bomas Draft (2004), which proposed a powerful Prime Minister and a four-tier devolution system. This was followed by the Wako Draft (2005), which was famously rejected in the 'Orange vs Banana' referendum due to concerns over executive power.

The process eventually culminated in the 2010 Promulgation, which settled on a pure Presidential system checked by a bicameral parliament and 47 County Governments. This shift established Constitutional Supremacy and specialized courts like the ELC.

Sources relied upon:
• Constitution of Kenya (2010); Proposed New Constitution (Wako Draft, 2005); Draft Constitution (Bomas Draft, 2004).
